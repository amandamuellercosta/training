Hip thrust progression — core growth driver
Hip thrusts are the main glute strength driver in this plan. Monday is your heavy day. Saturday is your pump day at 60–70% of Monday's weight. Treat them differently.
Monday — heavy strength
1.
Start at a weight you can do 4×6 with perfect form. If unsure, go lighter — form matters more than weight here.
2.
Work that weight until you hit 4×8 comfortably — usually takes 1–3 sessions.
3.
Add 5 kg and reset back to 4×6. Repeat the cycle.
4.
Every rep: drive through heels, full hip extension at top, 1 sec hold. No half reps.
5.
Every 4–5 weeks, deload to 60% of your current weight for one week, then push past your previous best.
Saturday — pump + shape
1.
Use 60–70% of Monday's weight. If Monday is 80 kg, Saturday is 48–56 kg. This is not a light day — it's a different stimulus.
2.
Target 3×12–15 reps. Start at 12 and progress to 15 before adding weight.
3.
Every rep: 2 sec squeeze at the top. Slow and controlled. Feel the glute, not the lower back.
4.
Progress reps first (12 → 15), then increase weight by 2.5–5 kg and reset to 12.
5.
Saturday weight tracks Monday — as Monday goes up, Saturday goes up proportionally.

Weight progression strategy
The rule
Hit the top of your rep range for all sets with good form, two sessions in a row → increase weight next session. Simple, repeatable.
Compound lifts
Hip thrusts, RDLs, squats, rows, press → +2.5–5 kg. Never sacrifice depth or form. Slow eccentrics on RDLs and hamstring curls — always.
Isolation moves
Kickbacks, curls, lateral raises, pushdowns → +1–2 kg. Small jumps matter. If the next dumbbell is too heavy, use a band to bridge the gap.
Glute machines
Abduction machine, cable kickbacks → +2–5 kg. Strong squeeze at the top every rep. Can't feel your glute? Drop the weight — the load is bypassing the muscle.
Deload — every 4–5 weeks
Drop all weights 20% for one week. Same exercises and reps — just lighter. This is when your body consolidates gains. Non-negotiable.
Plateau fix
Stuck 3+ sessions? Switch rep scheme (4×8 instead of 3×12), drop 10%, rebuild over 2 weeks. Or add a pause rep at the top to increase time under tension.
